# Demo Comic Strip: "Las Aventuras del Super Profe"

## Overview

A 6-panel comic strip to use as an icebreaker at the LangTeach demo. It tells the story of an overwhelmed language teacher who discovers LangTeach, the "super assistant" that saves his day.

**Tools needed:**
- Midjourney ($10/month Basic plan, cancel after) for image generation
- Canva (free) for assembly, text bubbles, and export

**Estimated time:** ~1.5 hours total

---

## Part 1: Generate Images with Midjourney

### 1.1 Sign up

1. Go to [midjourney.com](https://www.midjourney.com)
2. Click "Sign Up" and create an account (you can use Google or Discord)
3. Choose the **Basic Plan** ($10/month)
4. After payment, you land on the Midjourney image editor

### 1.2 How prompts work in Midjourney

- At the bottom of the page there is a **prompt bar** (text input)
- Paste your prompt, then press **Enter**
- Midjourney generates **4 image variations** (takes ~30 seconds)
- Hover over the one you like best and click it
- Click **"Upscale"** (or the U1/U2/U3/U4 buttons) to get full resolution
- Click the **download arrow** to save the image
- Name each file `panel1.png`, `panel2.png`, etc.

### 1.3 Style prefix

Add this at the **beginning** of every prompt to keep a consistent style:

```
Retro 1980s European comic panel, ligne claire technique, bold black ink outlines,
flat vibrant colors, comedic exaggerated expressions, cartoon proportions, humorous tone.
```

Add this at the **end** of every prompt for correct format:

```
--ar 3:2 --style raw
```

The `--ar 3:2` gives a landscape panel ratio. The `--style raw` prevents Midjourney from over-stylizing.

### 1.4 Character consistency tip

After generating Panel 1, if you like how the teacher character looks:
- Click on the image to open it
- Copy the image URL (right-click > Copy image address)
- In subsequent prompts, paste the URL at the beginning, then add your prompt. This helps Midjourney keep the character similar across panels.

Example:
```
https://cdn.midjourney.com/your-panel1-url.png Retro 1980s European comic panel... [rest of prompt]
```

## Part 3: Assemble in Canva

### 3.1 Sign up

1. Go to [canva.com](https://www.canva.com)
2. Sign up with your Google account or email (free plan is enough)

### 3.2 Create the presentation

1. Click **"Create a design"** (purple button, top right)
2. In the search bar type **"Presentation (16:9)"** and select it
3. You get a blank white slide

### 3.3 Title slide (Slide 1)

1. Click **"Text"** in the left sidebar
2. Click **"Add a heading"**
3. Type: **Las Aventuras del Super Profe**
4. Click the text, then click the **font name** in the top toolbar
5. Search for **"Bangers"** or **"Luckiest Guy"** (comic-style fonts)
6. Make the text large (60-80pt) and center it
7. Optionally add a subtitle: *"Una historia de supervivencia docente"*

### 3.4 Panel slides (Slides 2-4)

Use **2 panels per slide** for readability during a presentation:

**Slide 2: Panels 1 + 2**
1. Click **"+"** at the bottom to add a new slide
2. Click **"Uploads"** in the left sidebar
3. Click **"Upload files"** and select `panel1.png` and `panel2.png`
4. Drag `panel1.png` to the left half of the slide, resize to fill
5. Drag `panel2.png` to the right half
6. Add a thin black border: click **"Elements"** > search **"rectangle frame"** > place over each panel

**Slide 3: Panels 3 + 4**
- Same process with `panel3.png` and `panel4.png`

**Slide 4: Panels 5 + 6**
- Same process with `panel5.png` and `panel6.png`

### 3.5 Add speech bubbles

For each panel:

1. Click **"Elements"** in the left sidebar
2. Search **"speech bubble"** (or **"thought bubble"** for Panel 3)
3. Pick a white comic-style bubble
4. Drag it onto the panel, position it near the character's mouth
5. **Double-click** the bubble to type the text from the prompts above
6. Adjust font: select the text, change font to **"Bangers"**, size 14-18pt
7. Resize the bubble to fit the text

**For Panel 6's caption box:**
1. Search **"Elements"** > **"rectangle"**
2. Place a yellow/beige rectangle at the bottom of the panel
3. Add text on top: "LangTeach, tu super asistente para ensenar idiomas"

### 3.6 Final touches

- Make sure all panels have consistent border thickness
- Check that text is readable (not too small)
- Add the LangTeach logo if you have one (upload it and place on the title slide and Panel 6)

### 3.7 Export

**For presenting on screen:**
1. Click **"Share"** (top right)
2. Click **"Download"**
3. Format: **PNG** or **PDF**
4. Click **"Download"**

**For presenting directly from Canva:**
1. Click the **"Present"** button (top right, play icon)
2. Choose **"Present full screen"**
3. Use arrow keys to advance slides

## Part 4: Alternative Panel Layout (Single Page Comic)

If you prefer a single-page comic strip (for printing or sharing as an image):

1. In Canva, instead of "Presentation", create a **Custom size: 1200 x 1800 px** (portrait)
2. Place the title at the top
3. Arrange all 6 panels in a 2x3 grid (2 columns, 3 rows)
4. Add speech bubbles to each panel
5. Add the caption at the bottom
6. Export as PNG
