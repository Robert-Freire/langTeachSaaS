## Detailed Profiles

### Twee
**URL:** twee.com
**Pricing:** Free (20 text / 10 media runs/mo) · Pro $19/mo or $15/mo annual · Schools annual (custom)

**What they do well:**
- 30+ AI tools purpose-built for English teaching (reading, writing, speaking, listening, grammar, vocabulary)
- Generate exercises from YouTube videos (MCQs, true/false, gap fills)
- CEFR-calibrated output (A1-C2)
- Multimedia input: generate from video, audio, image, or URL
- Smart grading support for open-ended answers
- Export to PDF
- No credit card required for trial

**What they lack:**
- English only — no support for Spanish, French, or other languages
- No lesson planning workflow (tools are isolated, not structured into a lesson)
- No student profiles or persistent student context
- No content library / snippet reuse
- Media tool runs capped even on Pro
- Small team (10 people), slower feature velocity

**Threat level:** High — closest ELT-specific AI competitor. If they add multi-language support and a lesson workflow, the gap narrows significantly.

---

### Fluentize
**URL:** app.fluentize.com
**Pricing:** $3.49/lesson · $14.99 (5 lessons) · $49/6mo · $79/year · School plans $45-60/teacher/year

**What they do well:**
- High-quality curated video-based lesson plans (news, commercials, documentaries)
- 10-15 new lessons added monthly
- Printable worksheets, interactive PDFs, Google Slides versions
- Teacher guide included
- Co-branding option for schools

**What they lack:**
- Not a generative AI tool — content is pre-made and not customizable
- No CEFR-specific filtering
- Limited to video-based lessons
- No grammar, writing, or vocabulary exercise generation
- Per-lesson pricing is expensive if not subscribing

**Threat level:** Low — different product category (curated library vs. AI generator). Useful for inspiration on content quality bar.

---

### Curipod
**URL:** curipod.com
**Pricing:** Free core · Schools custom

**What they do well:**
- AI generates interactive slide-based lessons from a topic prompt
- Real-time student participation (polls, word clouds, drawings)
- AI learning insight reports (misconceptions, top understandings)
- 100% student participation model

**What they lack:**
- Not ELT-specific (no CEFR, no language-level differentiation)
- No writing, grammar, or vocabulary exercise generation
- Primarily a classroom presentation tool, not a materials creator
- No use for freelance 1-on-1 teachers

**Threat level:** Low — different use case (classroom interactive presentation vs. materials creation for 1-on-1 teaching).

---

### Eduaide.ai
**URL:** eduaide.ai
**Pricing:** Free (15 gen/mo) · Pro $5.99/mo or $49.99/year · Schools custom

**What they do well:**
- 110+ resource types (lesson plans, assessments, games, graphic organizers, jigsaw, leveled readings)
- Supports 15+ languages
- Pedagogical Knowledge Graph linking tools to teaching strategies
- Evidence-based instructional frameworks embedded
- Very competitive price ($5.99/mo)

**What they lack:**
- No CEFR level support
- No ELT methodology (communicative approach, skills-based sequencing)
- No student profiles
- No lesson planner workflow
- Language features are translation-focused, not ELT pedagogy-focused
- Overwhelming 110+ tool interface with no ELT-specific filtering

**Threat level:** Medium — broad coverage at low price. Teachers who want "just something cheap and fast" may land here. Differentiate on ELT specificity and workflow.

---

### MagicSchool AI
**URL:** magicschool.ai
**Pricing:** Free · Plus $8.33/mo (billed annually $99.96) · Enterprise custom

**What they do well:**
- 80+ teacher tools and 50+ student tools
- Text Leveler (rewrites content at different reading grades)
- Vocabulary Scaffolder with tiered lists and definitions
- Translation into any language
- Strong data privacy compliance (FERPA, COPPA, SOC 2, Common Sense Privacy 93%)
- Raina AI chatbot for assessment and rephrasing

**What they lack:**
- Grade-based leveling, not CEFR
- No ELT-specific exercise types (no speaking prompts, listening activities, communicative tasks)
- No lesson workflow for language teaching
- Oriented toward US school districts, not freelance ELT teachers
- 80+ tools with no ELT-focused filtering — discovery problem

**Threat level:** Low-Medium — likely to be adopted by school districts but not by independent Preply/iTalki teachers.

---

### Teachy
**URL:** teachy.ai
**Pricing:** Free tier · Premium individual (not public) · Premium School / Enterprise (custom)

**What they do well:**
- 60+ AI tools for lesson materials, slides, assignments, quizzes, grading
- 1 million+ peer-reviewed ready-to-use materials
- Multi-model AI (GPT, Claude, Gemini blended per task)
- Automated essay grading with personalized feedback
- No-prompt-needed generation interface
- Strong in LATAM / Brazilian market

**What they lack:**
- No CEFR support
- No ELT methodology
- Pricing opacity (individual pricing not public)
- Peer-reviewed materials quality is inconsistent
- Less validated in European/Asian ELT contexts

**Threat level:** Low — different market focus and no ELT specialization.

---

### Wordwall
**URL:** wordwall.net
**Pricing:** Free (limited) · Individual plans from ~$5.99/mo

**What they do well:**
- Extremely popular in ELT for interactive activities (matching, quizzes, gap-fills, drag-and-drop)
- Large template library
- Share activities with students via link
- Multi-language support

**What they lack:**
- Not AI-native (template-based, not generative)
- No lesson planning workflow
- No content generation
- No student profiles

**Threat level:** Low as a direct competitor, but high as a habitual tool — many teachers already use Wordwall for exercises. Consider interoperability or "better than Wordwall for generation" positioning.

---

### ESL Brains
**URL:** eslbrains.com
**Pricing:** Subscription (exact pricing not public)

**What they do well:**

**What they lack:**

- Limited to B1-C1 levels
- English only
- No customization

**Threat level:** Low — aspirational quality benchmark, not a direct competitor.

## Market Gaps (Our Opportunity)

Based on the competitor landscape, no current tool combines all of:

1. **CEFR-calibrated AI generation** (Twee does this for English only)
2. **Multi-language support** for teacher-created content (Spanish, French, etc.)
3. **Lesson planning workflow** (structured sections, templates, draft/publish)
4. **Student profiles** with persistent context that pre-fills generation
5. **Content library** for reusing and remixing generated snippets
6. **Integrated PDF export** (teacher view + student worksheet)

The closest competitor (Twee) covers 1 and partially 6, but misses 2, 3, 4, and 5 entirely.
